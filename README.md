# data-mining-for-health-care-applications

This study examines the use of data mining techniques
in healthcare, focusing on three important areas: clustering,
disease prediction, and large data analytics using association
rule mining. In a review of six peer-reviewed publications published
between 2022 and 2025, we examine how these techniques
are being used to uncover hidden patterns in medical data,
predict chronic diseases, and address the practical constraints of
working with large-scale healthcare datasets. Our findings show
that K-means clustering consistently outperforms other clustering
methods for patient grouping and uncovers new patterns and
trends in medical data. Random Forest remains the most reliable
algorithm for disease prediction tasks, and association rule
mining offers interpretability that traditional machine learning
models do not. In all three categories, data quality is identified as
the most significant impediment to advancement. The report also
analyzes the limitations of present methodologies and suggests
areas for future research, such as smarter rule filtering, standardized
data frameworks, and the incorporation of interpretable
models into clinical decision support systems.
